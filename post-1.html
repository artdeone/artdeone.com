5 Common Mistakes Beginners Make in Illustrator

Posted on September 26, 2025

Starting with Adobe Illustrator can be incredibly exciting. The possibilities seem endless, but it's also easy to fall into common traps that can slow down your progress. Here, we'll discuss five of the most frequent mistakes beginners make and how you can avoid them.

1. Not Using the Pen Tool Correctly

The Pen Tool is arguably the most powerful tool in Illustrator, but it has a steep learning curve. Many beginners try to "draw" with it by clicking many points, resulting in jagged, unprofessional lines. The key is to use as few anchor points as possible, focusing on creating smooth Bézier curves by clicking and dragging to create handles.

2. Working in RGB for Print Projects

This is a critical technical mistake. If you are designing something that will be printed, like a business card or a flyer, you must set your document's color mode to CMYK. If you design in RGB (which is for screens), the colors will look different and often duller when printed.
